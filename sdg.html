The 17 SDGS

What are the SDGs?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global goals established by the United Nations in 2015 to address various challenges such as poverty, inequality, and climate change, aiming for a better and more sustainable future by 2030. Each goal has specific targets and indicators to measure progress, focusing on areas like health, education, and environmental sustainability.
